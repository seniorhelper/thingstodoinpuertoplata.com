🏆 Quick Answer — What Are The Best Things To Do In Puerto Plata?

Puerto Plata, Dominican Republic offers some of the Caribbean's most thrilling adventures. The crown attraction is the 27 Charcos de Damajagua — 27 natural waterfall cascades where you hike, jump, slide, and swim through turquoise jungle pools. Above the city, Mount Isabel de Torres is home to the only aerial cable car (teleférico) in the entire Caribbean, climbing 2,600 feet to a Christ statue and botanical garden with panoramic Atlantic views. Beyond that: ATV and buggy tours rip through mountain trails, zip lines launch over jungle canopy, horseback rides trace the shoreline at sunset, Dudu Lagoon hides underground caves with crystal-blue pools, and Cayo Arena — a tiny coral sandbar island — floats just offshore for the best snorkeling on the North Coast.

🔥 Bucket-List Experiences

Top Things To Do In
Puerto Plata

Whether you're an adrenaline junkie hunting waterfalls or a curious traveler exploring colonial history, Puerto Plata delivers more adventure per square mile than anywhere else on the Caribbean coast.

⭐ #1 Must-Do
Moderate

27 Damajagua Waterfalls

Hidden in the sugarcane hills north of Santiago, the 27 Charcos de Damajagua are Puerto Plata's undisputed crown jewel. A 30–40 minute jungle hike brings you to a series of 27 cascading natural pools carved into luminous turquoise rock. You hike up, then jump, slide, and swim your way back down — one of the most genuinely exciting natural adventures in the entire Caribbean.

⏱️ ~4–5 hours 📍 ~45 min from PP 🏊 Bring swimwear

🌍 Only in Caribbean
Easy

Cable Car — Mount Isabel de Torres

The Caribbean's only aerial tramway climbs 2,600 feet up the lush slopes of Mount Isabel de Torres in a breathtaking 10-minute ride. At the summit: a towering Christ statue, botanical gardens with thousands of orchids, and some of the best panoramic views in the Dominican Republic.

⏱️ 2–3 hours 📍 City center 👨‍👩‍👧 Family-friendly

Dudu Lagoon — Caves & Blue Water

Dudu Lagoon is an electric-blue freshwater cenote surrounded by jungle, with limestone caves full of stalactites, swimming pools, and a zip line that drops you straight into the blue water below. One of the most striking natural environments on the entire North Coast.

🤿 Best Snorkeling
Easy

Cayo Arena — Paradise Island

Cayo Arena (Paradise Island) is a small white sandbar on a coral reef near Punta Rucia, ringed by turquoise waters teeming with tropical fish. The finest snorkeling spot on the North Coast. Catamaran tours include a stop at the famous natural swimming pool.

⏱️ Full day 📍 ~1.5 hr west 🐠 Snorkel included

🌊 Underwater

Scuba Diving & Snorkeling — Sosúa

Just 15 minutes east of Puerto Plata, Sosúa Beach sits on a protected bay with calm, crystal-clear water. The underwater landscape features coral formations, seahorses, sea turtles, and rich reef fish. Scuba schools here cater to all levels.

⏱️ Half-day 📍 15 min east 🐢 Beginner OK

Catamaran & Party Boat Tours

Spend a full day on the open Caribbean — dancing, snorkeling at coral reefs, swimming at the natural pool, and finishing with a sunset return as the Atlantic turns gold. Most boats include open bar and a crew that knows how to make memories.

⏱️ Full day 🎉 Groups 🍺 Open bar

🏛️ History & Culture
Easy

Fort San Felipe & Colonial Downtown

Built in 1577 to defend against pirates, Fortaleza San Felipe stands at the Malecón with original cannons, prison cells, and sweeping ocean views. Combine with a walk through the Victorian downtown, Amber Museum, and the famous Umbrella Street.

⏱️ 2–4 hours 📍 City center 📸 Umbrella Street
💎
🏆 Unique to DR
Easy

Amber Museum — World's Finest

Puerto Plata's Amber Museum, in a Victorian mansion downtown, holds one of the world's most remarkable amber collections — with prehistoric insects, lizards, and plant matter preserved in resin millions of years old. Surprisingly captivating for all ages.

⏱️ 1–2 hours 📍 Downtown 🧒 All ages
🪁
🌊 Day Trip
Moderate

Kitesurfing in Cabarete

Just 30 minutes east, Cabarete is the Caribbean's #1 kitesurfing destination with 250–300 windy days per year. Over 10 IKO-certified schools teach beginners through advanced freestyle. The beachfront bar scene is worth visiting even without a kite.

⏱️ Full day 📍 30 min east 🏄 Lessons available

🗺️ Know Before You Go

Puerto Plata, Dominican Republic:
The Essential Guide

Puerto Plata — officially San Felipe de Puerto Plata — sits on the Dominican Republic's northern Atlantic coast and has welcomed visitors since Columbus anchored here in 1493. Nicknamed "the Bride of the Atlantic," it's the adventure capital of the North Coast, a region known as the Amber Coast.

Today, Puerto Plata welcomes visitors through Luperón International Airport (POP) and two cruise ports — Amber Cove and Taino Bay — both minutes from downtown and the adventure circuit. 8 hours or 8 days, Puerto Plata rewards every visitor.

🍽️ Food & Culture

Taste The Dominican
North Coast

Between adventures, Puerto Plata's food scene serves up the real Dominican Republic — fresh-caught Atlantic fish, cacao from local mountain farms, and staples that locals have cooked for centuries.

🍌

Mangú

The Dominican breakfast of champions. Creamy mashed green plantains topped with pickled red onions, fried cheese, salami, and eggs. Order it at any local comedor.

🐟

Fresh Grilled Fish

The Atlantic delivers fresh mahi-mahi, snapper, and grouper daily to Puerto Plata's waterfront restaurants. Order whole fish, grilled with tostones and a cold Presidente.

🥘

Sancocho

The Dominican national stew — slow-cooked broth with multiple meats, root vegetables, and plantain. Served at celebrations and Sundays, absolutely worth seeking out at a local kitchen.

🍫

Artisan Cacao & Chocolate

The Puerto Plata mountains grow some of the world's finest cacao. Several operators offer bean-to-bar chocolate-making tours of women-run cooperatives that have produced chocolate for generations.

🥤

Morir Soñando

Literally "to die dreaming" — this iconic Dominican drink blends fresh orange juice with cold milk and sugar. Found at street carts throughout the city, and as refreshing as it sounds.

🎭

Merengue & Bachata Culture

Puerto Plata's Malecón comes alive at night with merengue, and Cabarete hosts the North Coast's best Latin dance nights. Bachata was born in the DR — hearing it live by the ocean is unforgettable.

💡 Insider Advice

Puerto Plata
Travel Tips

A few things experienced travelers wish they'd known before arriving on the North Coast.

01

Go to 27 Waterfalls Early

The 27 Waterfalls get busy mid-morning when cruise ships are in port. Arrive at opening time for smaller groups, better photos, and cooler temperatures for the hike.

02

Don't Drink Tap Water

Tap water is not safe for drinking or brushing teeth. Bottled water is cheap and available everywhere. Hotels and restaurants use safe water — confirm when dining locally.

03

USD Works Almost Everywhere

US Dollars are accepted at most tourist businesses. Dominican Pesos (DOP) get better rates at local spots. ATMs are widely available, including at the airport and downtown.

04

Take a Motoconcho for Short Trips

Motorcycle taxis (motoconchos) are cheap and ubiquitous for short city trips — agree on the price first. For Cabarete or Sosúa, private taxis or shared guagua buses are better.

05

November–April Is Peak Season

November–April brings the most reliable weather and most visitors. June–August is peak kite season in Cabarete. September–October sees the most rainfall.

06

Learn a Few Spanish Words

English is common at tourist spots, but a few Spanish phrases — "buenos días," "gracias," "cuánto cuesta?" — open more doors and build more goodwill than any translation app.
